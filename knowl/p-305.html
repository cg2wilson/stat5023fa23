A mixed effects model for \(i=1,...,a\text{,}\) \(j=1,...b\text{,}\) and \(k=1,...,n\) is given by
\begin{equation*} Y_{ijk} = \mu + \alpha_i + \beta_j + (\alpha\beta_{ij}) + \varepsilon_{ijk} \end{equation*}
where \(Y_{ijk}\) is the k-th observation at \(A=i,B=j\text{,}\) \(\mu\) is the overall population mean, \(\alpha_i\) is a fixed (non-random) main effect of factor \(A=i\text{,}\) \(\beta_j\) is a main effect of factor \(B=j\) such that \(\beta_j \sim^{iid} N(0,\sigma_\beta^2)\text{,}\) \((\alpha\beta)_{ij}\) is the interaction of \(A=i,B=j\) such that \((\alpha\beta)_{ij}\sim^{iid} N(0,\sigma_{\alpha\beta}^2)\text{,}\) and \(\varepsilon_{ijk}\sim^{iid} N(0,\sigma_\varepsilon^2)\) is a random error.
