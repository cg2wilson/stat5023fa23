Suppose that we want to test
\begin{equation*} H_0: \mu_i - \mu_j = 0 \end{equation*}
vs.
\begin{equation*} H_0: \mu_i - \m_j \neq 0 \end{equation*}
Our \(t-\)test statistic is given by
\begin{equation*} t = \dfrac{\bar{Y_{i\cdot}} = \bar{Y_{j\cdot}}}{\sqrt{MSE\cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{1}{n_i} + \dfrac{1}{n_j}}}}\sim t_{n_\cdot - t} \end{equation*}
under \(H_0\text{.}\) This gives a rejection region \(|t| \geq t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t}\text{,}\) which is equivalent to
\begin{equation*} |\bar{Y_{i\cdot}} - \bar{Y_{j\cdot}}| \geq t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t} \sqrt{MSE \cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{1}{n_i} + \dfrac{1}{n_j}}} \end{equation*}
here, we call the term
\begin{equation*} t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t} \sqrt{MSE \cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{1}{n_i} + \dfrac{1}{n_j}}} \end{equation*}
the least significant difference (LSD).
