Recall that \(SSC_L\) is a sum of squares associated with contrast \(L\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} SSC_L = \dfrac{\lrpar{\sum_{i=1}^t a_i\bar{Y}_{i\cdot}}^2}{\sum_{i=1}^t a_i^2/n_i} \end{equation*}
with a single degree of freedom. It can be shown that SSTrt from one-way ANOVA can be decomposed into \(t-1\) independent \(SSL_i\)s corresponding to each mutually orthogonal contrast. Given a set of mutually orthogonal contrasts \(L_1,L_2,...,L_{t-1}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} SSTrt = SSC_{L_1} + SSC_{L_2} + \cdots + SSC_{L_{t-1}} \end{equation*}
As shown above, we have
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{SSC_{L_i}/1}{MSE}\sim F_{1,n_\cdot - t} \end{equation*}
under the null hypothesis \(H_0: L_i = 0\)
