This method can be used for a general contrast; suppose we want to test
\begin{equation*} H_0: \mu_1 - \dfrac{1}{2}(\mu_5 + \mu_6) = 0 \end{equation*}
under \(\alpha = 0.05\text{.}\) We have
\begin{equation*} \hat{L} = 505 - \dfrac{1}{2}(600 + 470) = -30 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} S = \sqrt{5\cdot F_{0.05,5,24}}\cdot \sqrt{2451(1^2 + (-0.5)^2 + (-0.5)^2)/2} = 98.1449 \end{equation*}
We fail to reject \(H_0\text{,}\) which implies that the mean of Group 1 is not significantly different from that of Groups 5 and 6. The 95% C.I. is
\begin{equation*} -30 \pm 98.1449 = (-128.1449,68.149) \end{equation*}
The fact that 0 is included in the C.I. implies that there is no significant difference.
