and
\begin{align*} S_2 \amp = \sqrt{(b-1)\cdot F_{\alpha,b-1,ab(n-1)}}\cdot \sqrt{\dfrac{MSE}{na}\sum_j a_j^2} \\ \amp = \sqrt{2\cdot F_{0.05,2,12}}\cdot \sqrt{\dfrac{42.39}{8}\cdot \lrpar{(-1)^2 + 2^2 + (-1)^2 }} \\ \amp = 15.72 \end{align*}
Now, calculate each \(\hat{L_i}\) and compare \(|\hat{L_i}|\) with \(S_i\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \hat{L_1} = (\overline{Y_{1\cdot\cdot}} + \overline{Y_{2\cdot\cdot}}) - (\overline{Y_{3\cdot\cdot}} + \overline{Y_{4\cdot\cdot}}) = -9.17 \end{equation*}
So \(|\hat{L_1}\lt S_1\text{.}\) Similarly,
\begin{equation*} \hat{L_2} = 2\overline{Y_{\cdot 2\cdot}} - \lrpar{\overline{Y_{\cdot 1\cdot}}+\overline{Y_{\cdot 3 \cdot}}} = 31.49 \end{equation*}
So \(|\hat{L_2}| \gt S_2\text{.}\) We have two conclusions:

    We reject \(H_{02}\text{,}\) which implies that the average yield of the 2nd variety is significantly different from the average yield of the 1st and 3rd varieties.
