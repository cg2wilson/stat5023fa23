Example 10.2.1.

A company wants to evalute the effectiveness of two different sunscreen types (\(S1\) and \(S2\)) with 40 subjects. For each subject, a 1-inch square was marked off on their back, under the shoulder and above the small of the back. A response is recorded based on reading the color of the skin in the square before and after the application of a fixed amount of each sunscreen. The measurement of color is extremely variable, so the company randomly selects 10 technicians from their worldwide staff to participate in the study to assess the variability in the readings due to the technician taking the readings. Four subjects, two having \(S1\) and two having \(S2\text{,}\) were randomly assigned to each technician for evaluation.
  1. Write the appropriate model for the study
  2. Write the appropriate hypotheses which can be tested by using the following table:
    Source df SS MS EMS
    Suncreen 1 4.49 4.49 \(\sigma_\varepsilon^2 + 2\sigma_{\alpha\beta}^2 + 20\sum_i \alpha_i^2\)
    Technician 9 517.49 57.50 \(\sigma_\varepsilon^2 + 2\sigma_{\alpha\beta}^2 + 4 \sigma_\beta^2\)
    Interaction 9 5.97 0.66 \(\sigma_\varepsilon^2 + 2\sigma_{\alpha\beta}^2\)
    Error 20 2.64 0.13 \(\sigma_\varepsilon^2\)
    Total 39
  3. Test the hypotheses specified in part (b)
  4. Make a conclusion:
    1. Reject \(H_0: \sigma_{\alpha\beta}^2=0\text{,}\) thus we would conclude that the variations in the determinations of skin color due to technician differences are different for the two types of sunscreen.
    2. Reject \(H_0: \sigma_\beta^2 = 0\text{,}\) thus there is a significant source of random variation due to variability from technician to technicia
    3. Reject \(H_0: \alpha_1-\alpha_2 = 0\text{,}\) thus there is significant evidence that the mean response differs for the two sunscreens
  5. Estimate the variance components by using the method of moments.
