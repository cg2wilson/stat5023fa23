This assumes the balanced case, i.e. \(n_1 = n_2 = \cdots =n_t = n\text{.}\) We replace the LSD term
\begin{equation*} LSD = t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t} \sqrt{MSE \cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{2}{n}}} \end{equation*}
with
\begin{equation*} HSD = q_{\alpha,t,n_\cdot - t}\sqrt{\dfrac{MSE}{n}} \end{equation*}
where \(q_{\alpha,t,n_\cdot - t}\) is a critical value found in a Tukey’s HSD table. The term
\begin{equation*} q_{\alpha,t,n_\cdot - t}\sqrt{\dfrac{MSE}{n}} \end{equation*}
is called Tukey’s Honest Significant Difference (HSD). We reject the hypothesis \(H_0: \mu_i - \mu_j = 0\) if \(|\bar{Y_{i\cdot}} - \bar{Y_{j\cdot}}|\geq HSD\text{.}\) The \(100(1-\alpha)\%\) Tukey C.I. for \(\mu_i - \mu_j\) is given by
\begin{equation*} (\bar{Y_{i\cdot}}-\bar{Y_{j\cdot}}) \pm HSD \end{equation*}
Tukey’s method provides a valid type I error control.
