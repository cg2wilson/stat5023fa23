Recall the "effects" model for one-way ANOVA such that
\begin{equation*} Y_{ij} = \mu + \tau_i + \varepsilon_{ij} \end{equation*}
where \(Y_{ij}\) is the \(j-\)th observation of treatment level \(i\text{;}\) \(\mu \text{ and }\tau_i\) are the overall mean and the \(i-\)the treatment level effect; and \(\varepsilon_{ij}\) is the random error associated with \(Y_{ij}\) such that \(\varepsilon_{ij}\approx^{i.i.d.} N(0,\sigma^2)\)
