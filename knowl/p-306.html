Recall that, for the average difference among \(t\) treatment levels, we test the hypothesis
\begin{equation*} H_0: \tau_1 = \tau_2 = \cdots \tau_t = 0 \end{equation*}
against \(H_a:\) at least one \(\tau_i\) is non-zero. Under \(H_0\text{,}\) the EMS for the treatment should be the same with that for error; under \(H_a\text{,}\) the EMS for treatment should be larger than that for error. This means that for the test statistic
\begin{equation*} F = \dfrac{MSTrt}{MSE}\sim F_{t-1,t(n-1)} \end{equation*}
under \(H_0\text{,}\) an F-value closer to 1 indicates greater likelihood that we fail to reject \(H_0\text{.}\)
