Consider an \(a\times b\) factorial treatment structure in a CRD. The fixed effects model for \(i = 1,...,a\text{,}\) \(j = 1,...,b\text{,}\) and \(k = 1,...,n\) is given by
\begin{equation*} Y_{ijk} = \mu + \alpha_i + \beta_j + (\alpha\beta)_{ij} + \varepsilon_{ijk} \end{equation*}
where \(Y_{ijk}\) is the k-th observation at \(A = i,B=j\text{,}\) \(\mu\) is the overall population mean, \(\alpha_i\) is a fixed (non-random) main effect factor of \(A = i\text{,}\) \(\beta_j\) is a fixed (non-random) main effect factor of \(B = j\text{,}\) \((\alpha\beta)_{ij}\) is a fixed (non-random) interaction of \(A = i,B=j\text{,}\) and \(\varepsilon_{ijk}\sim^{iid} N(0,\sigma^2_\varepsilon)\) is a random error.
