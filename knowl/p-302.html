Consider a one-way treatment structure with \(t\) levels in a CRD. Then we have the fixed effects model
\begin{equation*} Y_{ij} = \mu + \tau_i + \varepsilon_{ij} \end{equation*}
where \(i = 1,...,t\) and \(j = 1,...,n\text{,}\) \(Y_{ij}\) is the \(j-\)th observation in treatment level \(i\text{,}\) \(\mu\) is the overall mean (a fixed constant), \(\tau_i\) is the \(i-\)the level effect (a fixed constant), and \(\varepsilon_{ij}\) is random error associated with \(Y_{ij}\) such that \(\varepsilon_{ij}\sim^{iid} N(0,\sigma^2_\varepsilon)\text{.}\) Note that there are only \(t\) levels about which we are making inferences.
