A main effect is the effect of each individual treatment/factor on the response. For example, the main effect of treatment \(A\) is the average of simple effects of \(A\text{.}\) In the nitrogen/phosphorous example, the estimated main effect of nitrogen is given by
\begin{equation*} \overline{Y_{2\cdot\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{1\cdot\cdot}} = \dfrac{1}{4}\lrpar{\sum_{i,j=1}^{i=2,j=2}Y_{2ij}} - \dfrac{1}{4}\lrpar{\sum_{i,j=1}^{i=2,j=2} Y_{1ij}} \end{equation*}
Here, the main effects are the differences in the mean response of a factor over all other levels of a factor.
