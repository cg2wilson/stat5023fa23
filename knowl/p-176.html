For a \(100(1-\alpha)\%\) confidence interval for \(\mu_i - \mu_j\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} (\bar{Y_{i\cdot}} - \bar{Y_{j\cdot}}) \pm t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t} \sqrt{MSE \cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{1}{n_i} + \dfrac{1}{n_j}}} \end{equation*}
so that in the balanced case, we have
\begin{equation*} LSD = t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t} \sqrt{MSE \cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{2}{n}}} \end{equation*}
In general, the LSD makes pairwise comparison and is recommended to use when one-way ANOVA gives a significant result; it does not control FWER.
