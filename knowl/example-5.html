Example 3.1.3.

Researchers conducted a study of biological agents to assess their effectiveness in comparison to the chemical weed agents. The study consisted of a control (no agent), two biological agents (Bio1 and Bio2) and two chemical agents (Chm1 and Chm2). Thirty 1-acre plots of land were planted with hay. Six plots were randomly assigned to receive one of the five treatments. The hay was harvested, and the total yield in tons per acre was recorded for each plot.
None (Control) Bio1 Bio2 Chm1 Chm2
\(\bar{Y}_{i\cdot}\) 1.175 1.293 1.328 1.415 1.500
\(s_i\) 0.1204 0.1269 0.1196 0.1249 0.1265
\(n_i\) 6 6 6 6 6
We can ask these questions:
  1. Is there a difference of effectiveness between control and the average of all agents?
    \begin{equation*} L_1 = \mu_1 - \dfrac{1}{4}\lrpar{\mu_2 + \mu_3 + \mu_4 + \mu_5} \end{equation*}
    NOTE: The choice of \(a_i\) in a contrast is arbitrary; provided that \(\sum a_i = 0\text{,}\) we can choose
    \begin{equation*} \tilde{L_1} = 4\mu_1 - \lrpar{\mu_2 + \mu_3 + \mu_4 + \mu_5} \end{equation*}
  2. Is there a difference of effectiveness between Bio1 and the average of all chemical agents?
    \begin{equation*} L_2 = \dfrac{1}{2}(\mu_4 + \mu_5) - \mu_2 \end{equation*}
  3. Is there a difference of effectiveness between Bio2 and the average of all chemical agents?
    \begin{equation*} L_3 = \dfrac{1}{2}(\mu_4 + \mu_5) - \mu_3 \end{equation*}
  4. Compare biological agents 1 and 2?
    \begin{equation*} L_4 = \mu_2 - \mu_3 \end{equation*}
