An experiment with repeated measures is one in which each experimental unit has more than a single measurement taken over time or location. For example, if patients are randomly assigned to one of two medications, they may have a response measured every week for three weeks. Comparison of medications, weeks on medications, and the interaction between the two can be investigated. Let \(Y_{ijk}\) denote an observation at time \(j\) on experimental unit \(k\) of treatment level \(i\text{.}\)
