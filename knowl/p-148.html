The degrees of freedom for \(SS_{H_0}\text{,}\) denoted \(df_{H_0}\text{,}\) is the minimum number of contrasts required to express the simultaneous equality of means. In the weed control example, \(df_{H_0} = 3\text{;}\) in general, we have
\begin{equation*} F = \dfrac{SS_{H_0}/df_{H_0}}{MSE} \approx F_{df_{H_0},n_\cdot - t} \text{ under }H_0 \end{equation*}
where \(MSE = SSE/(n_\cdot - t\) comes from one-way ANOVA. In this situation, we reject \(H_0\) if \(F \geq F_{\alpha,df_{H_0},n_\cdot -t}\)
