The presence of significant, fixed interaction means that there are different effects of the factor A across the levels of the other factor, B. In a mixed model, the levels of factor B are randomly selected; however, random interaction in a mixed model is to account for variability of our response attributed to each combination of factors A and B. No matter what the results are for the test of \(\sigma_{\alpha\beta}^2\text{,}\) we can proceed to use the F-tests for factors A and B.
