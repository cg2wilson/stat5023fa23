Note in particular that the test statistics are constructed differently. Unlike the fixed effects model, the result from testing \(\sigma_\alpha^2 = 0\) and \(\sigma_\beta^2 = 0\) can be interpreted even when there exists significant evidence in favor of \(\sigma_{\alpha\beta}^2 \gt 0\text{.}\) If any variance components turn out to be significant, then we are interested in estimating the variance components (see below).
