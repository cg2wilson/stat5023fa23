A completely randomized design with \(a\times b\times c\) factorial treatment structure can be described by the means model
\begin{equation*} Y_{ijkl} = \mu_{ijk} + \varepsilon_{ijkl} \end{equation*}
where \(\varepsilon_{ijkl}\tilde^{iid} N(0,\sigma^2)\) is random error and \(\mu_{ijk}\) is the population mean with \(A=i,B=j,C=k\text{,}\) or the effects model
\begin{equation*} Y_{ijkl} = \mu + \alpha_i + \beta_j + \gamma_k + (\alpha\beta)_{ij} + (\alpha\gamma)_{ik} + (\beta\gamma)_{jk} + (\alpha\beta\gamma)_{ijk} + \varepsilon_{ijkl} \end{equation*}
We have the main effects \(\alpha_i,\beta_j,\gamma_k\text{,}\) two-way interactions \((\alpha\beta)_{ij},(\alpha\gamma)_{ik},(\beta\gamma)_{jk}\text{,}\) and three-way interaction \((\alpha\beta\gamma)_{ijk}\)
