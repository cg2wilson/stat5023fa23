Consider the mutually orthogonal contrasts below:
\begin{equation*} H_0: \mu_2 - \mu_3 = 0,\quad \mu_2 + \mu_3 -2\mu_4 = 0 \quad \mu_2 + \mu_3 + \mu_4 - 3\mu_5 = 0 \end{equation*}
The above contrasts are simultaneous contrasts (which also happen to be mutually orthogonal). When all such contrasts are 0, it follows that \(\mu_i = \mu_j\) for appropriate \(i,j\) in the null hypothesis considered.
