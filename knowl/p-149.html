In the weed example, we can compute:
\begin{align*} SSC_{L_1} \amp = 0.0037 \\ SSC_{L_2} \amp = 0.0437 \\ SSC_{L_3} \amp = 0.1076 \end{align*}
This means that
\begin{align*} F \amp = \dfrac{SS_{H_0}/3}{MSE} \\ \amp = \dfrac{(0.0037 + 0.0437 + 0.1076)/3}{0.0153}\\ \amp = 3.3769 \end{align*}
which is compared to \(F_{3,25}\) under \(H_0\text{.}\) Since \(F \geq F_{0.05,3,25} = 2.99\text{,}\) we reject \(H_0\text{.}\) Thus, under \(\alpha = 0.05\text{,}\) there is evidence that at least one average effect on weed control is different among the four agents.
