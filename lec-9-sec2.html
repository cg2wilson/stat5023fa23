    Section 5.2 Fisher‚Äôs LSD

    Suppose that we want to test
    \begin{equation*} H_0: \mu_i - \mu_j = 0 \end{equation*}
    vs.
    \begin{equation*} H_0: \mu_i - \mu_j \neq 0 \end{equation*}
    Our \(t-\)test statistic is given by
    \begin{equation*} t = \dfrac{\overline{Y_{i\cdot}} = \overline{Y_{j\cdot}}}{\sqrt{MSE\cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{1}{n_i} + \dfrac{1}{n_j}}}}\sim t_{n_\cdot - t} \end{equation*}
    under \(H_0\text{.}\) This gives a rejection region \(|t| \geq t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t}\text{,}\) which is equivalent to
    \begin{equation*} |\overline{Y_{i\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{j\cdot}}| \geq t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t} \sqrt{MSE \cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{1}{n_i} + \dfrac{1}{n_j}}} \end{equation*}
    here, we call the term
    \begin{equation*} t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t} \sqrt{MSE \cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{1}{n_i} + \dfrac{1}{n_j}}} \end{equation*}
    the least significant difference (LSD).
    For a \(100(1-\alpha)\%\) confidence interval for \(\mu_i - \mu_j\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} (\overline{Y_{i\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{j\cdot}}) \pm t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t} \sqrt{MSE \cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{1}{n_i} + \dfrac{1}{n_j}}} \end{equation*}
    so that in the balanced case, we have
    \begin{equation*} LSD = t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t} \sqrt{MSE \cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{2}{n}}} \end{equation*}
    In general, the LSD makes pairwise comparison and is recommended to use when one-way ANOVA gives a significant result; it does not control FWER.

    Example 5.2.1.

    Six population means were tested by one-way ANOVA, which indicates there is at least one different mean. Using \(\alpha = 0.05\text{,}\) \(MSE = 2451\text{,}\) and \(n_i = 5\) for all \(i\text{,}\) make all pairwise comparisons using Fisher‚Äôs LSD.
    Group 1 2 3 4 5 6
    Sample Mean 505 528 564 498 600 470
    Solution.
    First, compute the LSD:
    \begin{equation*} LSD = t_{\alpha/2,n_\cdot - t}\sqrt{MSE\cdot \lrpar{\dfrac{2}{n}}} = t_{0.025,24}\sqrt{2451\lrpar{\dfrac{2}{5}}} = 64.6235 \end{equation*}
    Second, sort the sample means from each group in increasing order:
    Group 6 4 1 2 3 5
    Sample Mean 470 498 505 528 564 600
    Compare the smallest to the largest, second largest, etc. until we find a non-significant pair:
    \begin{align*} |\overline{Y_{6\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{5\cdot}}| \amp = 102 \gt LSD\\ |\overline{Y_{6\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{3\cdot}}| \amp = 94 \gt LSD\\ |\overline{Y_{6\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{2\cdot}}| \amp = 30 \lt LSD \end{align*}
    Continue with Group 4, Group 1 and Group 2:
    \begin{align*} |\overline{Y_{4\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{5\cdot}}| \amp = 102 \gt LSD\\ |\overline{Y_{4\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{3\cdot}}| \amp = 66 \gt LSD \\ |\overline{Y_{4\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{2\cdot}}| \amp = 30 \lt LSD \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} |\overline{Y_{1\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{5\cdot}}| \amp = 95 \gt LSD\\ |\overline{Y_{1\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{3\cdot}}| \amp = 59 \lt LSD \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} |\overline{Y_{2\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{5\cdot}}| \amp = 72 \gt LSD\\ |\overline{Y_{2\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{3\cdot}}| \amp = 36 \lt LSD \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} |\overline{Y_{3\cdot}} - \overline{Y_{5\cdot}}| \amp = 36 \lt LSD \end{align*}
    Now, identify groups without significant difference:
    • Groups 6,4,1,2 do not differ
    • Groups 1,2,3 do not differ
    • Groups 3,5 do not differ
    This gives the following table:
    Group 6 4 1 2 3 5
    Sample Mean \(470^a\) \(498^a\) \(505^{ab}\) \(528^{ab}\) \(564^{bc}\) \(600^c\)
    The sample means with a common superscript do not significantly differ under \(\alpha = 0.05\text{.}\)
